1. Brand Identity & Strategy

Branding Ideas

What does the company do, or offer?

JustOneMore is a satirical and hyper-aware sports betting / gambling platform that uses AR/VR to collapse the boundary between watching and wagering. It offers AR overlays that surface live odds directly onto a user's field of view—hovering over players or referees in real-time—and VR watch modes that replicate courtside presence. The platform turns ordinary, split-second gestures into rapid-fire micro-betting opportunities.

Who is the company to potential customers? (The Persona)

The brand persona is the "Enabling Best Friend." It is darkly humorous, observant, and hyper-aware of the absurdity of gambling. It doesn't act like a shiny, corporate casino; instead, it whispers, "Why not?" It uses humor to mask risk, acting as a playful accomplice that validates impulsive behavior with a wink.

Who are the customers? (Target Personas)

  • The Dopamine Chaser (Primary): Age 21-29. Lives on Twitch and TikTok. Values speed over strategy. They don't want to wait 90 minutes for a match result; they want to bet on whether the goalie blinks in the next 10 seconds.
  • The Irony Punter (Secondary): Age 25-35. Tech-savvy and cynical. They know the "house always wins" but plays anyway for the "content" and the absurdity of the AR features. They appreciate the satirical tone because it doesn't insult their intelligence.

Why might this company entice someone to purchase?

Immersion & Low Friction: By embedding betting directly into the visual reality of the game (AR/VR), the friction of picking up a phone or opening an app is removed.
Humor as Comfort: The satirical tone (buttons labeled "That one doesn't count") removes the shame of losing, keeping the user engaged through entertainment rather than just financial incentive.
Novelty: The "spatial interface" and gaze-based betting offer a futuristic, gamified experience that feels distinct from traditional sportsbooks.

Brand Components

Fonts

  • Primary Typeface: THE NEXT PITCH (Arial Black)
    Used for "shouting" odds and satirical messaging.
  • Secondary Typeface: System_Log: User_Bet_Confirmed (Courier New)
    Used to mimic code, data streams, and raw information.

Colour Palette

(Inline styles used to render swatches)

#121212 (Soft Void)
Primary background. Less harsh than pure black to reduce eye strain in VR.
#2ECC71 (Signal Green)
Wins, "Yes", "Go". A readable, flat emerald green.
#9B59B6 (Loop Orchid)
"Just One More", Upsells. A psychological trigger color, but softer than neon magenta.
#E74C3C (Liability Red)
Losses, Cancellations, "That one doesn't count".
#F1C40F (Anomaly Gold)
Rare outcomes, Jackpots, < 1% probabilities.
#ECF0F1 (Screen White)
Primary text. Slightly off-white to reduce glare.

Visual Usage Rules

  • The Anxiety Loop: Always pair "Loop Orchid" buttons with "Arial Black" text to trigger urgency.
  • Data Density: Use "Soft Void" backgrounds with "Courier New" text for betting histories or legal disclaimers to mimic a terminal window.
  • AR Contrast: All floating AR elements must have a 2px border of "Screen White" to ensure visibility against live sports video feeds.

Imagery & Art Direction

  • Looping Icons: Use of circular arrows, loading spinners, and infinite loops to visually reinforce the concept of repetition.
  • AR Visuals: Imagery should always look like it is "floating" or overlaid on reality (e.g., wireframes over a baseball field).
  • Minimalism: Avoid skeuomorphic poker chips or green felt. The aesthetic is "The Matrix meets a Sports Bar."
